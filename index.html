Org-scope STAR stories (staff/lead)

~90m advanced drill 2027-02-11 to 2027-02-28

Goal. Draft and rehearse stories that show cross-team influence, ambiguity navigation, and setting technical direction, not just "I executed on my team."

Sources
  • Tech Interview Handbook: behavioral for senior candidates : the senior-vs-staff scope framing and STAR structure.

Exercise. Draft 4-5 STAR stories, each with the org-scope signal explicit ("shaped the decision / created alignment / managed cross-team risk", not "executed"). Suggested set: (1) drove a technical direction across two or more teams; (2) navigated an ambiguous problem with no clear owner; (3) influenced without authority (a decision that was not yours to make); (4) managed cross-team or release risk that could have shipped a regression; (5) mentored or leveled up other engineers / set a standard (e.g. an AI code-review rubric). Write Situation-Task-Action-Result, then say each out loud once.

Blog angle. "STAR stories for staff-level interviews: the scope words that actually move the bar"

Done when. each story has a one-line org-scope hook and you can tell it in under two minutes without notes.
